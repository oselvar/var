# Roman numbers

Each row gives an example of a decimal and a roman number:

| decimal | roman     | Rule illustrated                                                                |
| ------: | :-------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
|       0 | *N*       | Medieval notation for zero (*nulla*); classical Romans had no numeral for zero. |
|       3 | III       | `I`, `X`, `C`, and `M` may be repeated up to three times.                       |
|       4 | IV        | Subtractive notation: `I` before `V` means 5−1.                                 |
|       9 | IX        | `I` may also precede `X` to mean 10−1.                                          |
|      40 | XL        | `X` may precede `L` to mean 50−10.                                              |
|      90 | XC        | `X` may precede `C` to mean 100−10.                                             |
|     400 | CD        | `C` may precede `D` to mean 500−100.                                            |
|     944 | CMXLIV    | Multiple subtractive groups can occur in the same numeral.                      |
|    1984 | MCMLXXXIV | Combination of additive and subtractive notation.                               |
|    2500 | MMD       | Large values are typically additive: 1000 + 1000 + 500.                         |
